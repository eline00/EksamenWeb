Universal design

Automatic sliding doors are, for example, a universal design we all encounter daily. The concept is simple, doors that open automatically by sensors detecting that someone is in front and opening without assistance. This way, people in wheelchairs, the blind, the elderly, children and more can get in and out of places without a problem. Not only is it easier for users with disabilities, but also for us. Often our hands are full of different things, and if we had to put down everything we hold each time to grab a door handle it would have been cumbersome, but with automatic sliding doors we do not encounter this problem. Given the current corona situation, this is more hygienic so that diseases are reduced.

Another example is the elevator, which is a means of transport for people and is an alternative way to get to different floors. It is a modern and easily accessible design that everyone encounters on a daily basis and can be used by any user. Elevators are most suitable for the disabled, the elderly and pregnant women and have often adapted functions that in turn adapt to the user's needs, such as shutter speed and buttons with Braille.

We use the elevator everyday, for example at school in situations with heavy bags or that teaching is on the highest floor, so our everyday life becomes easier and we save energy and time from having to walk / use stairs.

The latest universal design we have chosen is low floor . Low floors mean that the floor on different public transport is at the same level as the pavement the vehicle is next to. This is assigned to people who have challenges getting up from the sidewalk and up to the vehicle. This can prevent injuries, accidents or passengers being forgotten. We meet this design in everyday life, which makes it easier for us to get on the collective. We do not need to take into account the height difference between the vehicle and the pavement, and do not think about any damage caused by the gap in between.

Universal design helps different people in everyday life and improves the quality of life. Both automatic sliding doors, lifts and low floors are modern designs that help people in everyday life without making a clear distinction in society, and which we ourselves use every day.
